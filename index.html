How hotels are valued — and what you actually keep

Value starts with income

Hotels are usually valued on the income approach: stabilized NOI divided by a market capitalization rate. A property earning $1,000,000 of NOI at an 8.5% cap rate is worth roughly $11.8M. Value moves on two things you can influence — your NOI — and one you can't — where cap rates sit in your market. Knowing a realistic number before you talk to brokers or buyers keeps the conversation grounded.

The sale price is not your payday

What you keep is what's left after the capital stack is paid. From the price, subtract the senior loan payoff, any mezzanine or preferred equity, prepayment penalty or defeasance, and sale costs. What remains is your net proceeds. If debt and preferred equity exceed today's value, your common equity is underwater — and a straight sale may not even clear the debt, which changes the conversation entirely.

Sell or refinance?

If the property comfortably supports its debt and you have a real equity cushion, refinancing usually preserves your upside. If there's a large refinance gap or your cushion is thin, a planned sale can beat a forced refinance under a maturity deadline. The diagnostic quantifies both sides in dollars — your net proceeds today versus what refinancing and holding would require — so the decision is math, not emotion.

Questions, answered

How much will I actually keep when I sell?
Roughly: value, minus the loan payoff, minus any preferred equity, minus prepayment or defeasance and sale costs. The diagnostic's waterfall shows exactly where your equity stands at a given value.
What is a cap rate, and what's a good cap rate for a hotel?
A capitalization (cap) rate = NOI ÷ property value, and it's the main way income properties are valued: value = NOI ÷ cap rate. Hotel cap rates vary by market, segment, and cycle — often roughly 7–10% — and a lower cap rate implies a higher value. Example: $1,000,000 NOI at an 8.5% cap rate ≈ $11.8M value.
How does my capital stack affect my proceeds?
Everyone ahead of you — senior lender, mezzanine, preferred equity — is paid in full before you see a dollar. The more layers and the thinner your cushion, the less you keep. The tool ranks the stack and shows your position.
What if I owe more than it's worth?
Then your common equity is underwater and a straight sale may not cover the debt. A negotiated payoff, a recap, or holding and improving NOI first come into play — we can help you weigh them.
Should I sell or refinance?
If the property comfortably supports its debt and you have cushion, refinancing keeps your upside. If there's a large refinance gap or you're thin, a clean sale can beat a forced refinance. The diagnostic quantifies both.
Do prepayment penalties really matter?
They can be large on CMBS (defeasance) and some fixed-rate loans (yield maintenance), and they come straight out of your proceeds — factor them in before you assume a number.
