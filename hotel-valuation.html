How are hotels valued?

The dominant method is the income approach: a property's value equals its stabilized net operating income divided by a market capitalization (cap) rate. It reflects what an investor would pay for that income stream. Replacement-cost and sales-comparison approaches exist too, but for an operating hotel, income rules.

The cap rate formula, with an example

Value = NOI ÷ cap rate, and cap rate = NOI ÷ value. Example: a hotel with $1,000,000 of NOI valued at an 8.5% cap rate is worth 1,000,000 ÷ 0.085 ≈ $11.8M. Raise NOI by $100,000 at the same cap rate and value rises about $1.18M — which is why operations drive value.

What is a good cap rate for a hotel?

Hotel cap rates vary widely by market, segment, brand, and where we are in the cycle — often roughly 7–10%, higher than most other property types because hotel income is more volatile. A lower cap rate means a higher value, so a buyer paying a low cap rate is paying up for stability or growth.

Questions, answered

How do you value a hotel?
Most commonly by the income approach: value = stabilized NOI ÷ market cap rate. Example: $1,000,000 NOI at an 8.5% cap rate ≈ $11.8M.
What is a cap rate?
A capitalization rate = NOI ÷ property value, expressed as a percent. Rearranged, value = NOI ÷ cap rate. It's the market's required return on the income.
What is a good cap rate for a hotel?
It varies by market, segment, and cycle — often roughly 7–10%, typically higher than other property types because hotel cash flow is more volatile. Lower cap rate = higher value.
How much is my hotel worth?
